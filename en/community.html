Who is using SMART Health Cards?

A small sampling of the ever-growing number of companies, organizations, and public entities that have adopted SMART Health Cards.

Canada

In October 2021, Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau announced the launch of a standardized SMARTH Health Card-compliant vaccination verification system that all provinces and territories will be adopting nationwide.

Japan

Introduction of Japan's digital vaccine certificate is planned by the end of 2021, with a 2D barcode issued in accordance with international standards, including SMART Health Cards, ICAO, and VDS-NC.

California

The State of California is offering residents a digital record of their COVID-19 vaccination status.

Louisiana

The State of Louisiana adopted SMART Health Cards in their LA Wallet app, which provides residents digital vaccination verification, sharing of personal health records, and functions as a legal driver’s license.

New York

New York State has developed interoperable solutions for use nationwide with their Excelsior Pass Plus and Excelsior Scanner app for businesses, both of which adhere to the SMART Health Cards Framework.

Apple

With iOS 15.1, iPhone users can securely store verifiable versions of their COVID-19 vaccination and test result records in the Health app and as vaccination cards in Apple Wallet. These records are based on the SMART Health Cards specification.

Meet the organizations behind SMART Health Cards development and adoption.

Boston Children’s Hospital’s Computational Health Informatics Program is the home of SMART. BCH research and innovation goes beyond pediatrics, and it counts more than 1,100 scientists—including nine members of the National Academy of Sciences—among its research community.

MITRE’s mission-driven teams are dedicated to solving problems for a safer world. Through our public-private partnerships and federally funded R&D centers, we work across government and in partnership with industry to tackle challenges to the safety, stability, and well-being of our nation.

SMART connects people to their clinical data by transforming electronic health records into platforms that can be used with apps. It’s open, free and standards-based API (application programming interface) allows a developer to write and app once and have it run anywhere in the healthcare system.

VCI is a voluntary coalition of more than 900 public and private organizations committed to empowering individuals access to a trustworthy and verifiable copy of their vaccination records in digital or paper form using the SMART Health Cards open, interoperable standard.
