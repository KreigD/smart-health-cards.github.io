SMART Health Card Adopters

A small sampling of the ever-growing number of companies, organizations, and public entities that have adopted SMART Health Cards.

Walmart

Walmart is creating a digital wallet for its customers to track their health information.

University of California Health

The University of California Health is rolling out a SMART Health Card solution for vaccines and test results to help students and faculty return to school safely in the fall.

State of California

The State of California is offering residents a digital record of their COVID-19 vaccination status.

Quebec Province

Nearly 5 million residents of the Province of Quebec have downloaded the Akinox Pandemic Response Platform, chosen by the province to help reduce the impact of COVID-19.

Apple

Apple has adopted SMART Health Cards in its Health App so people can share health data with doctors and family members.

Meet the organizations that developed SMART Health Cards.

Boston Children’s Hospital’s Computational Health Informatics Program is the home of SMART. BCH research and innovation goes beyond pediatrics, and it counts more than 1,100 scientists–including 9 members of the National Academy of Sciences–among its research community.

SMART connects people to their clinical data by transforming electronic health records into platforms that can be used with apps. It’s open, free and standards-based API (application programming interface) allows a developer to write and app once and have it run anywhere in the healthcare system.
