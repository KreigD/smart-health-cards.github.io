Technical Specifications

What are SMART Health Cards?

SMART Health Cards are paper or digital versions of your clinical information, such as vaccination history or test results. They allow you to keep a copy of your records on hand and share this information with others if you choose.

Your SMART Health Card may be printed or digital

How do SMART Health Cards Work?

Get it

You might receive a paper or digital SMART Health Card from any organization that has your clinical information, such as a pharmacy, doctor’s office, or state registry.

If you haven’t received one, you can request it through that organization’s website or a compatible app.

Learn more about getting a SMART Health Card.

Save it

You can keep a SMART Health Card on paper or as a file on your phone. You can also make copies for safe-keeping. If you are a parent or a caregiver, you can keep SMART Health Cards for yourself or for others, just as you do with other clinical information.

Learn more about the benefits of having your clinical information on hand.

Share it

You can share a SMART Health Card with others if you choose. For example, you might share a it to show your vaccine status for school registration or to travel. You share your SMART Health Card by letting someone scan the 2D barcode (“QR code”) on your paper or phone screen. You send it as a file or through a phone app.

Learn more about choosing to share your SMART Health Card.

SMART Health Cards and Privacy

SMART Health Cards contain the minimal information required to display your COVID-19 vaccination and/or test status, and the choice to share your Card is up to you.

SMART Health Cards contain:

  • Your legal name and date of birth
  • Your clinical information
    • Tests: date, manufacturer, and result
    • Vaccinations: type, date, and location

They should not contain:

  • Your phone number
  • Your address
  • Your government-issued identifier
  • Any other health information

SMART Health IT

The SMART Health Cards Project is a program from SMART Health IT, a group of public and private organizations working with leaders at Boston Children’s Hospital to connect people with their health data.

Its immediate goal is to aid a safe return to pre-COVID 19 activities by giving people access to their test results and vaccine records and, if they choose, the ability to share that information.
